Haunted Stops — Paranormal Territory
San Jose, CA
Winchester Mystery House
160 rooms built by a widow haunted by victims of the Winchester rifle. Doors open to walls, stairs lead to ceilings. Evening flashlight tours and basement access available.
Evening flashlight tours
Alameda, CA
USS Hornet
America's most decorated and most haunted aircraft carrier. Over 300 crew died aboard. Ghostly sailors and flickering lights widely reported. Overnight stays in original sailors' bunks.
Overnight stays available
Ione, CA
Preston Castle
Gothic reform school built in 1890, haunted by former wards and a housekeeper murdered in the 1950s. Featured on Ghost Hunters and Ghost Adventures. Bring your own ghost-hunting gear.
Paranormal investigation nights
Sacramento, CA
Delta King Riverboat Hotel
Historic paddleboat moored on the Sacramento River, haunted by its original captain. Phantom footsteps on deck and glimpses in the halls at night. Stay the night on board.
Stay the night on board
Eureka, CA
Fort Humboldt
Remote military fort overlooking Humboldt Bay. The ghost of a commander who died in 1897 reportedly stares from hospital windows at passing hikers. Best experienced at dusk.
Day visits
Antioch, CA
Black Diamond Mines
A white apparition hovers in the foggy twilight above this abandoned coal mining preserve. Overnight camping available nearby for those wanting to stay close to the darkness.
Camping nearby
— Bonus Destinations — Bigfoot & Cryptid Country —
Orleans, CA  |  Six Rivers National Forest
Bluff Creek — Patterson-Gimlin Film Site
The holy grail of Bigfoot research. On 20 October 1967, Patterson and Gimlin filmed the world's most famous cryptid footage here. A short hike brings you to the exact sandbar.
Daylight hike required
Willow Creek, CA  |  Humboldt County
Willow Creek China Flat Museum
Self-proclaimed Bigfoot capital of the world. Home to footprint casts, sighting maps, newspaper archives and decades of field evidence. Gateway to the Bigfoot Scenic Byway.
Free entry, open year-round
